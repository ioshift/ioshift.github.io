Sum of Euler's Totient Function By Group Theory

2024-05-03 3 min read # solved

Problem

Prove

n=∑d∣nφ(d)n=\sum_{d\mid n}\varphi(d) n=d∣n∑​φ(d)

Solution

We first prove a lemma

A finite cyclic group GGG of order nnn has exactly one subgroup of each order ddd dividing nnn, and that these are all subgroups it has.

Proof.
Suppose G=⟨a⟩G=\langle{a}\rangleG=⟨a⟩, then for all d∣nd\mid nd∣n, ⟨and⟩\langle{a^{\frac{n}{d}}}\rangle⟨adn​⟩ is a subgroup with order ddd. Assume there are other subgroups of GGG with order ddd generated by aka^kak (since a subgroup of a cyclic group must be cyclic as well), then we got

gcd⁡(n,k)=nd  ⟹  nd∣k  ⟹  ak∈⟨and⟩\gcd(n,k)=\frac{n}{d}\implies \frac{n}{d}\mid k\implies a^k\in\langle{a^{\frac{n}{d}}}\rangle gcd(n,k)=dn​⟹dn​∣k⟹ak∈⟨adn​⟩

Hence ⟨and⟩\langle{a^\frac{n}{d}}\rangle⟨adn​⟩ is the only group with order ddd. Also by the Theorem of Lagrange, there is no subgroup with order d′∤nd'\nmid nd′∤n. Therefore, all subgroups of GGG are

{⟨and⟩∣d∈Z+, d∣n}\{\langle{a^\frac{n}{d}}\rangle\mid d\in\Z^+,\, d\mid n\} {⟨adn​⟩∣d∈Z+,d∣n}

■\blacksquare■

We know that a cyclic group with order iii has φ(i)\varphi(i)φ(i) distinct generators, then ⟨and⟩\langle{a^\frac{n}{d}}\rangle⟨adn​⟩, as a subgroup of the group G=⟨a⟩G=\langle{a}\rangleG=⟨a⟩ of order nnn, has φ(d)\varphi(d)φ(d) generators. By the lemma above, since no two subgroups of GGG have the same generator, the number of generators of all subgroups of GGG is

∣{t∈G∣⟨t⟩≤G}∣=∑d∣nφ(d)\lvert\{t\in G\mid \langle{t}\rangle\leq G\}\rvert=\sum_{d\mid n}\varphi(d) ∣{t∈G∣⟨t⟩≤G}∣=d∣n∑​φ(d)

Notice the left hand side exactly equals nnn, then we obtain

n=∑d∣nφ(d)n=\sum_{d\mid n}\varphi(d) n=d∣n∑​φ(d)
