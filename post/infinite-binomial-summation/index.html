Infinite Binomial Summation

2024-03-29 4 min read # solved

Problem

Evaluate

∑i=n∞(in)⋅14i\sum_{i=n}^\infty\binom{i}{n}\cdot\frac{1}{4^{i}} i=n∑∞​(ni​)⋅4i1​

Solution

Let

f(n)=∑i=n∞(in)⋅14if(n)=\sum_{i=n}^\infty\binom{i}{n}\cdot\frac{1}{4^{i}} f(n)=i=n∑∞​(ni​)⋅4i1​

Notice

(in)=∑j=ni(j−1n−1)\binom{i}{n}=\sum_{j=n}^{i}\binom{j-1}{n-1} (ni​)=j=n∑i​(n−1j−1​)

Then we have

∑i=n∞(in)⋅14i=∑i=n∞∑j=ni(j−1n−1)⋅14i=∑j=n∞(j−1n−1)∑i=j∞14i=∑j=n∞(j−1n−1)14j−1⋅3=∑i=n−1∞(in−1)14i⋅3=13f(n−1)\begin{aligned} \sum_{i=n}^\infty\binom{i}{n}\cdot\frac{1}{4^{i}} &=\sum_{i=n}^\infty\sum_{j=n}^{i}\binom{j-1}{n-1}\cdot\frac{1}{4^{i}} \\ &=\sum_{j=n}^{\infty}\binom{j-1}{n-1}\sum_{i=j}^\infty \frac{1}{4^{i}}\\ &=\sum_{j=n}^\infty\binom{j-1}{n-1} \frac{1}{4^{j-1}\cdot 3}\\ &=\sum_{i=n-1}^\infty\binom{i}{n-1} \frac{1}{4^{i}\cdot 3}\\ &=\frac{1}{3}f(n-1) \end{aligned} i=n∑∞​(ni​)⋅4i1​​=i=n∑∞​j=n∑i​(n−1j−1​)⋅4i1​=j=n∑∞​(n−1j−1​)i=j∑∞​4i1​=j=n∑∞​(n−1j−1​)4j−1⋅31​=i=n−1∑∞​(n−1i​)4i⋅31​=31​f(n−1)​

Also know

f(0)=∑i=0∞14i=43f(0)=\sum_{i=0}^\infty \frac{1}{4^{i}}=\frac{4}{3} f(0)=i=0∑∞​4i1​=34​

Thus

f(n)=43n+1f(n)=\frac{4}{3^{n+1}} f(n)=3n+14​
