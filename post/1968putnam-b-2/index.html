Problem

Suppose that ⟨G,∗⟩\langle G,*\rangle⟨G,∗⟩ is a group with nnn elements, and A⊆GA\subseteq GA⊆G has more than n2\frac{n}{2}2n​ elements. Then for every g∈Gg\in Gg∈G, ∃a,b∈A\exists a,b\in A∃a,b∈A such that a∗b=ga*b=ga∗b=g.

Solution

Prove by contradiction. Assume the statement is false.

Let R=G/AR=G/AR=G/A, for every g∈Gg\in Gg∈G define a function f:A→Rf:A\to Rf:A→R by

f(a)=a−1gf(a)=a^{-1}g f(a)=a−1g

for all a∈Aa\in Aa∈A. Obviously fff is injective, hence we got ∣R∣≥∣A∣\left\vert R \right\vert \geq \left\vert A \right\vert∣R∣≥∣A∣. However, we have ∣A∣>n2\left\vert A \right\vert >\frac{n}{2}∣A∣>2n​ and thus ∣R∣<n2\left\vert R \right\vert <\frac{n}{2}∣R∣<2n​, which is a contradiction.

Hence ∃b∈A\exists b\in A∃b∈A such that a−1g=b  ⟹  g=a∗ba^{-1}g=b\implies g=a*ba−1g=b⟹g=a∗b.
