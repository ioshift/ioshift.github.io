Natural Number Is Ordered

2024-03-02 3 min read # solved

Problem

Prove N\NN is ordered.

Solution

Proof.
Equivalent to prove x≤yx\le yx≤y or y≤xy\le xy≤x for all x,y∈Nx,y\in\Nx,y∈N.
(P.S. Here a≤ba\le ba≤b defined to be ∃c∈N, b=a+c\exist c\in\N,\, b=a+c∃c∈N,b=a+c )

Use induction on xxx.

  • x=0x=0x=0
    notice y∈Ny\in\Ny∈N, x+y=y  ⟹  x≤yx+y=y\implies x\le yx+y=y⟹x≤y, done.
  • x=succ⁡(z)x=\operatorname{succ}(z)x=succ(z) (P.S. Here succ⁡\operatorname{succ}succ is the basic operator in Peano axioms how we construct N\NN)
    Known z≤yz\le yz≤y or y≤zy\le zy≤z, split into cases
    • z≤yz\le yz≤y
      ∃w∈N, y=z+w\exists w\in\N,\, y=z+w∃w∈N,y=z+w
      Consider cases of www.
      • w=0w=0w=0
        then y=zy=zy=z and x=succ⁡(z)≥z=yx=\operatorname{succ}(z)\ge z=yx=succ(z)≥z=y (since z+1=succ⁡(z)z+1=\operatorname{succ}(z)z+1=succ(z)), done.
      • w=succ⁡(v)w=\operatorname{succ}(v)w=succ(v)
        then y=z+succ⁡(v)=z+v+1=succ⁡(z)+v=x+v≥xy=z+\operatorname{succ}(v)=z+v+1=\operatorname{succ}(z)+v=x+v\ge xy=z+succ(v)=z+v+1=succ(z)+v=x+v≥x, done.
    • y≤zy\le zy≤z
      ∃w∈N, z=y+w\exist w\in\N,\, z=y+w∃w∈N,z=y+w
      Then x=succ⁡(z)=z+1=y+w+1≥yx=\operatorname{succ}(z)=z+1=y+w+1\ge yx=succ(z)=z+1=y+w+1≥y, done. ■\blacksquare■
