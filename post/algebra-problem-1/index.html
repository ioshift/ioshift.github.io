Solution

Prove by induction.

Base case: Obviously true for n=1n=1n=1

Inductive Step: Assume true for n=pn=pn=p, then for n=p+1n=p+1n=p+1

∑i=1p+1xi=∑i=1p+1yi  ⟹  ∃m(xm≥ym)\sum_{i=1}^{p+1}x_i=\sum_{i=1}^{p+1}y_i\implies\exists m(x_m\ge y_m) i=1∑p+1​xi​=i=1∑p+1​yi​⟹∃m(xm​≥ym​)

Define {an}\{ a_n \}{an​} and {bn}\{ b_n \}{bn​} by

an={xnn<mxm+xm+1n=mxn+1n>mandbn={ynn<mym+ym+1n=myn+1n>ma_n=\begin{cases} x_n & n<m \\ x_m+x_{m+1} & n=m \\ x_{n+1} & n>m \end{cases}\quad\text{and}\quad b_n=\begin{cases} y_n & n<m \\ y_m+y_{m+1} & n=m \\ y_{n+1} & n>m \end{cases} an​=⎩⎪⎨⎪⎧​xn​xm​+xm+1​xn+1​​n<mn=mn>m​andbn​=⎩⎪⎨⎪⎧​yn​ym​+ym+1​yn+1​​n<mn=mn>m​

Then it's equivalent to prove that ∃k\exist k∃k such that for all t∈{1,2,…,p}t\in\{ 1,2,\dots,p \}t∈{1,2,…,p}

∑i=1tak+i≥∑i=1tbk+i\sum_{i=1}^ta_{k+i}\ge \sum_{i=1}^tb_{k+i} i=1∑t​ak+i​≥i=1∑t​bk+i​

which is true by assumption.
