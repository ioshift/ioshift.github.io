Problem 1

∫sin⁡xsin⁡x+cos⁡xdx\int\frac{\sin x}{\sin x+\cos x}\mathrm{d}x ∫sinx+cosxsinx​dx

Solution

Consider

∫cos⁡xsin⁡x+cos⁡xdx\int\frac{\cos x}{\sin x+\cos x}\mathrm{d}x ∫sinx+cosxcosx​dx

We have

∫sin⁡xsin⁡x+cos⁡xdx+∫cos⁡xsin⁡x+cos⁡xdx=x+C\int\frac{\sin x}{\sin x+\cos x}\mathrm{d}x+\int\frac{\cos x}{\sin x+\cos x}\mathrm{d}x=x+C ∫sinx+cosxsinx​dx+∫sinx+cosxcosx​dx=x+C

We also have

∫sin⁡xsin⁡x+cos⁡xdx−∫cos⁡xsin⁡x+cos⁡xdx=∫−d(sin⁡x+cos⁡x)sin⁡x+cos⁡x=−ln⁡(sin⁡x+cos⁡x)+C\begin{aligned} \int\frac{\sin x}{\sin x+\cos x}\mathrm{d}x-\int\frac{\cos x}{\sin x+\cos x}\mathrm{d}x &=\int \frac{-\mathrm{d}(\sin x+\cos x)}{\sin x+\cos x} \\ &=-\ln(\sin x+\cos x)+C \end{aligned} ∫sinx+cosxsinx​dx−∫sinx+cosxcosx​dx​=∫sinx+cosx−d(sinx+cosx)​=−ln(sinx+cosx)+C​

Hence we obtain

∫sin⁡xsin⁡x+cos⁡xdx=x−ln⁡(sin⁡x+cos⁡x)2+C\int\frac{\sin x}{\sin x+\cos x}\mathrm{d}x=\frac{x-\ln(\sin x+\cos x)}{2}+C ∫sinx+cosxsinx​dx=2x−ln(sinx+cosx)​+C
